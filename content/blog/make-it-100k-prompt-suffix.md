AI builds to the average of its training data, and the average website is mediocre. To get above-average output, you have to *define what above-average means* — because the model won't reach for it on its own. Here's a reusable suffix you can paste onto the end of any UI prompt to do exactly that.

## The suffix

> Hold this to a premium, $100k-agency standard, not a default template. Specifically: use a strict spacing scale and let whitespace create hierarchy; pick three type weights with real contrast; keep the palette restrained (one accent, used sparingly); add considered motion — staggered entrances on scroll, eased hovers, a magnetic primary button — at 60fps and reduced-motion-aware; design the focus states, the selection color, the empty/loading states, and the microcopy with a human voice. Avoid the obvious AI tells: centered-everything layouts, the default purple gradient, emoji bullet points, and one uniform drop-shadow. Make deliberate choices a senior designer would defend.

Append it to your existing prompt. It works in v0, Cursor, Lovable, Bolt, Figma Make, and Claude.

## Why it works

A vague prompt gets a vague (average) result. This suffix does three things:

1. **Sets a reference point** ("$100k-agency standard") the model can aim at instead of the mean.
2. **Names the specific moves** that separate premium from default, so the model has concrete instructions, not a vibe.
3. **Names the tells to avoid** — the anti-patterns are as important as the patterns, because they're what the default *would* have done.

You're not hoping for good taste. You're spelling it out.

## Make it yours

Swap the specifics to match your brand. Building something playful? Change "restrained palette" to "bold, clashing color" and "eased hovers" to "springy, overshooting motion." The structure stays; the values flex. Keep a personal version in a snippet and append it to everything.

**The takeaway:** the model will happily build premium UI — it just won't *decide* to. Your job is to define "good" precisely enough that it can't default. This suffix is that definition.
