You built it. It works. Nobody's using it. This is the most common place a vibe-coded app dies — not because it's bad, but because building got easy and *distribution* didn't. Here's the sequence I'd run with no budget and no audience.

## 1. Build the last 20% in public

You're going to keep working on the app anyway. Do it where people can see. Post the before/after of one screen. Post the bug that took four hours. Post the decision you're stuck on and ask. You're not "marketing" — you're working with the door open. This compounds into an audience that's pre-sold by the time you launch.

## 2. Turn the product into content

Your app does something. Show it doing that thing, in a 15-second clip, doing it *better* than the obvious alternative. One feature, one clip, one sharp caption. This is the highest-converting content you can make because it's a demo and an ad and a proof-of-taste at once. (It's also why our [showcase](/showcase) uses live previews — the artifact *is* the marketing.)

## 3. Seed three niche communities, not the whole internet

Find three places your exact user already gathers — a subreddit, a Discord, a Slack. Don't drop a link. Be useful for two weeks: answer questions, share what you learned building. Then, when you mention what you made, it lands as a recommendation from a regular, not a drive-by ad. Ten engaged users from the right community beat a thousand strangers.

## 4. Ship one pSEO page type

Pick one templated page that answers a long-tail query your users search — "best X for Y", "X alternative", "how to do X in Z". Build it once, generate fifty. It's the only channel that keeps working while you sleep, and it's absurdly underused by technical founders. ([Here's how pSEO works for founders.](/prompts))

## 5. Make the first run worth screenshotting

Every new user is a potential distributor *if* the first thing they see is share-worthy. A delightful empty state, a result they want to show off, a moment of "oh, nice." Build virality into the product, not bolted on after.

**The takeaway:** the work isn't "get users." It's: be visible while you build, make the product into content, earn three communities, plant one SEO engine, and make the first run shareable. Do those five and 1,000 is a *floor*.

The first 1,000 is a playbook. The next 10,000 is the same playbook, executed with taste and consistency — which is the part most founders quietly skip. If you'd rather have someone run it with you, [that's the build](/build).
