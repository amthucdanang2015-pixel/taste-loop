Show me your easing curves and I'll tell you what your product feels like. Two apps can use the identical animation — a panel sliding in — and one feels like a toy, one feels like a bank, one feels like a $200/month luxury tool. The only difference is the *shape of the acceleration*. AI ships the default. Defaults have no personality.

## What easing actually is

Easing is how speed changes over the duration of a motion. Linear = constant speed, which never happens in the physical world, so it always feels robotic. Real things accelerate and decelerate. The *curve* of that change is a personality dial.

## The four personalities

- **Ease-out (fast start, soft landing).** The workhorse for things *entering*. Confident, responsive, modern. If you pick one default, pick this.
- **Spring (overshoots, then settles).** Playful, alive, energetic. Great for toys, social, consumer. Too much for enterprise.
- **Ease-in-out (slow, fast, slow).** Smooth and calm. Reads as premium and deliberate. Good for luxury and content-heavy products.
- **Sharp / anticipation (pulls back, then snaps).** Bold, characterful, a little theatrical. Neo-brutalism and playful brands live here.

Pick the one that matches what you *are*, and use it consistently. Consistency is what turns "some animations" into "a feel."

## How to direct it

You don't need the math. You need to name the personality and the entrance/exit:

> "Entrances ease-out over 300ms. The primary CTA hover uses a gentle spring. Exits are quicker than entrances (200ms). Keep it consistent everywhere."

That last rule matters: **exits should be faster than entrances.** Things should leave quickly and arrive gracefully — get this backwards and the whole product feels sluggish.

## The test

Record your app and scrub it frame by frame. If the motion has the same flavor everywhere and matches your brand's vibe, you have a personality. If it's a grab-bag of defaults, you have a template that happens to move.

**The takeaway:** color and type are what your brand looks like; easing is how it *moves*, and movement is the part people feel without noticing. Choose it on purpose.

This is the kind of decision baked into every [showcase](/showcase) prompt — the easing is specified, not left to chance, which is why the effects feel like *something* instead of generic.
