Designers obsess over spacing because it's where "amateur" and "professional" actually diverge. AI is bad at it in a consistent, recognizable way — it treats space as something to fill, not something to compose. Here are the five tells and the one rule that fixes them.

## 1. The same padding everywhere

AI applies one comfortable padding to everything, so nothing has hierarchy. Real layouts use space *as* hierarchy — more room around the important thing, less around the supporting cast. If every section breathes equally, nothing stands out.

## 2. No vertical rhythm

Gaps between sections are random: 40px here, 64px there, 48px somewhere else, with no system. Pick a spacing scale (4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64) and use *only* those values. Consistency reads as intention; randomness reads as generated.

## 3. Text packed to the edges

AI fills the container width with text, giving you 120-character lines that are exhausting to read. Cap body text at ~65–75 characters (`max-w-prose`). The whitespace on the sides isn't wasted — it's what makes the text readable.

## 4. CTAs floating in too much space

The opposite failure: a lonely button marooned in a giant empty section, disconnected from the thing it relates to. Proximity is meaning. Keep the button close to the copy that justifies it; group things that belong together.

## 5. Edge-to-edge density with no margin to think

Cards touching cards touching the viewport edge. No room for the eye to rest. Add generous outer margins and let the content sit *inside* the page, not bursting out of it. Premium layouts are confident enough to leave space empty.

## The one rule that fixes all five

**Space is a design element, not the absence of one.** Decide what each gap *means* — is this thing related to that thing, or separate? Group with tight space, separate with generous space, and use a single consistent scale. That one shift fixes most of what makes AI output feel cheap.

**The takeaway:** you don't add whitespace to make things pretty. You use it to tell the eye what goes with what. AI fills space; designers compose it.
