AI gets you to "it works" in an afternoon. Then you spend three months wondering why nobody sticks. The reason is that "it works in the happy path" and "it's a product" are separated by six things AI quietly skipped.

## The 6-step gap

**1. Empty states.** The first thing a real user sees is your app with *no data* — and AI always builds the full-data screen. An empty state that teaches and invites is the difference between activation and a bounce. Design the zero state first.

**2. Error and edge cases.** What happens when the API fails, the input is weird, the user does it in the wrong order? AI builds the demo path. Products survive the messy one. Every unhandled error is a user gone for good.

**3. Onboarding.** Your landing page gets them to sign up. Your onboarding decides if they ever come back. AI hands you a form, not a first-run experience. This is the highest-leverage screen in your app and it's usually missing.

**4. Performance under reality.** The prototype is fast with three rows. Real users bring a thousand. Pagination, loading states, optimistic updates — the boring work that makes an app feel solid instead of fragile.

**5. A retention loop.** Acquisition gets the first visit. A loop gets the second. AI doesn't ask "why would they open this tomorrow?" — and if you can't answer it, you don't have a product, you have a demo people tried once.

**6. A single, sharp reason to exist.** The prototype does five things adequately. A product does one thing better than the alternative. Cutting four features is harder than building them, and it's the work that decides whether anyone cares.

## How to close it

Don't rebuild. Audit. Walk your own product as a brand-new user with no data, on a slow connection, doing things in the wrong order. Write down every place it cracks. That list — not new features — is your roadmap to a real product.

**The takeaway:** AI builds the demo. The product is everything the demo lets you skip.
