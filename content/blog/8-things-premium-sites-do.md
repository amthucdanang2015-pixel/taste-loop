The gap between a template and a site that makes people say "who built this?" isn't one big thing. It's eight small ones, each invisible on its own and unmistakable together. AI skips all of them. Here they are.

## 1. Smooth scroll

Premium sites glide; default sites jerk. Add momentum scrolling (Lenis or a tuned CSS setup). It's the first thing the body feels before the eyes read anything.

## 2. Staggered entrances

Content fades and rises as it enters the viewport, slightly offset between siblings. One technique, applied site-wide, and suddenly everything looks *placed* instead of dumped.

## 3. Real focus states

Tab through your site. If the focus outline is the ugly browser default or missing entirely, that's a tell — and an accessibility fail. Design your focus states like you design your hovers.

## 4. A custom selection color

Highlight text on a premium site and the selection matches the brand. It's two lines of CSS (`::selection`) and it signals that someone sweated the details most people never notice.

## 5. Optimistic UI

When a user acts, the interface responds *immediately* and reconciles with the server after, instead of freezing on a spinner. This single pattern is most of why good apps feel "fast."

## 6. Empty states that teach

The screen before there's any data is the first real impression. Premium products make it a guide; AI gives you a blank panel. Design the zero state on purpose.

## 7. Microcopy with a voice

Button labels, error messages, tooltips — premium sites write these like a human with a personality, not a system. "Something went wrong" vs. "That didn't save — mind trying again?" Same function, completely different feel.

## 8. A loading state that isn't a spinner

Skeletons that match the content shape, a subtle shimmer, a progress hint — anything that says "this was designed." A bare spinner says "we stopped here."

**The takeaway:** premium isn't a budget. It's a checklist of small decisions, none of which AI makes for you by default. Add these eight and your site jumps a tier in an afternoon.
