You get one launch. Most founders waste it on a thread that opens with "Excited to announce…" and lists features to an audience that scrolls right past. Here's the structure that actually earns reach and routes people to the product — built from how the launches that *pop* are actually shaped.

## The skeleton

**Post 1 — the hook (the whole game).** No "excited to announce." Open with a *result*, a *tension*, or a *visual*. "I took my Lovable app from 2% to 18% activation in 30 days. Here's everything I changed 🧵" or a 10-second clip of the product doing its best trick. If post 1 doesn't earn the click on its own, the other nine don't exist.

**Post 2 — the stakes.** Why should they care? Name the pain you solve and who has it. Make the reader think "that's me."

**Posts 3–6 — show, don't tell.** One idea per post, each with a *visual* — a clip, a before/after, a screenshot. This is where you demonstrate the product *working*, not describe it. Threads live or die on whether every post has something to look at.

**Post 7 — proof.** A number, a testimonial, a result. One concrete piece of evidence that you're not just talking.

**Post 8 — the soft origin.** A line or two of why you built it. Humans share things from humans. Skip the corporate voice.

**Post 9 — the ask.** *One* clear CTA. Try it, join the list, reply with your use case. One. Multiple asks = zero conversions.

**Post 10 — the loop.** "I'll be posting what I learn as this grows — follow along." Turn one launch into an ongoing reason to follow you.

## The rules that matter more than the structure

- **Every post earns the next.** Write post 1, then ask "would I tap to see post 2?" If no, rewrite.
- **Visual > text, always.** A thread of text paragraphs gets skimmed. A thread of clips gets watched.
- **One idea per post.** Cramming kills rhythm. White space is a feature.
- **Reply to every reply for the first two hours.** Early engagement is what the algorithm reads as "show this to more people." Block the time.

## Reuse it

Repurpose the same skeleton for every milestone — a big feature, a result, a teardown. The structure is a template; only the artifacts change. (Same logic as turning [one teardown into a week of content](/blog).)

**The takeaway:** a launch thread is a story with a visual on every beat and one clear ask at the end — not a press release. Hook first, show don't tell, one CTA, and be in the replies.
