You can spot a v0 site in half a second. Not because v0 is bad — because everyone accepts the first output. The defaults are a fingerprint, and your users have seen it a hundred times this week.

Here are the seven tells, and the fix for each.

## 1. Everything is centered

The default layout centers every section in a single column. It reads as "template." Fix: give one section an asymmetric, off-grid moment — a left-aligned headline with a visual bleeding off the right edge. One break in the rhythm is enough.

## 2. The default gradient

That purple-to-blue diagonal is the new Comic Sans. Fix: replace it with either a flat, confident color or a *slow-moving* gradient mesh with grain over it. Movement and texture read as intentional; a static diagonal reads as untouched.

## 3. Inter at one weight

AI ships everything in Inter Regular. Fix: keep Inter if you like it, but use **three** weights with real contrast — a heavy display size for the headline, a normal body, and a medium for labels. Type contrast is 80% of "designed."

## 4. Emoji bullet points

🚀 ✨ 💡 in a feature list is the tell of tells. Fix: replace with real icons (one consistent set) or, better, a small custom visual per feature. Emoji say "I didn't have time." Icons say "I cared."

## 5. No motion at all

The page just… sits there. Fix: add three things — a staggered fade-up as sections enter the viewport, a magnetic hover on the primary button, and a smooth scroll. That's an afternoon, and it changes the entire felt quality.

## 6. Cards with the same shadow everywhere

One drop-shadow, applied to everything, flattens the page. Fix: use elevation as hierarchy — the thing you want clicked gets more lift; everything else gets almost none. Shadow should mean something.

## 7. Copy that lists features instead of making a promise

"AI-powered. Fast. Secure." Fix: one sentence that says what changes for the user. Features go below. Your headline is the most expensive real estate on the internet; don't spend it on adjectives.

**The takeaway:** the default is never the design. It's the starting point you were supposed to push off from.
